1.Hybrid structures

This narrative visualization follows an interactive slideshow.

2.Scenes

1)A choropleth template is used for visual consistency and the scenes follow the order of years.

2)Layout and structure

Choropleth map can best represent the unemployment rate variation among states. The range of legend is determined by the maximum and minimum values. A separate blue palette is used to distinguish states of different unemployment rates. The deeper the color, the higher the unemployment rate.

3.Annotation

All the annotations include the state names and the specific unemployment rate values. The annotations follow a same rectangular template containing this information and will appear when you put the mouse over the states. It will be cleared when you move the mouse out. All annotations will be cleared when switched to the next scene because unemployment rate will vary by year.

4.Parameters

The page addresses are parameters. Page 1 to page 6 represent year 2008 to year 2018, respectively. And they also control the current states which are the years when the data were collected.

5.Triggers

The year number buttons on the left side of the pages and the 'previous slide, next slide' buttons on the top of the charts are the triggers, which can change the page addresses to the selected year.

All the triggers are marked by the underlines which usually represent hyperlinks. Besides, the year number buttons are emphasized by being placed in the navigation plate which has a different background color. The ‘previous slide, next slide’ buttons are also marked by arrows, showing moving directions. Therefore, the viewer can be easily aware of available user events.
